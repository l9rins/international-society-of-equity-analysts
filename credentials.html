Credentials & Certification

The Certified Equity Analyst Program

A rigorous credentialing framework designed to signal competence, ethical reliability and analytical seriousness to employers, regulators and institutional investors.

Flagship Credential

The Certified Equity Analyst (CEA) Program

The Certified Equity Analyst designation is the credentialing foundation on which the Society's membership structure rests. It demonstrates, in a manner that is verifiable rather than assumed, that a practitioner can perform equity analysis to a standard consistent with the responsibilities the profession carries.

Candidates progress through a curriculum built around the technical and analytical demands of modern equity markets: financial statement interpretation, valuation methodology, sector and macroeconomic analysis, corporate governance assessment, and the ethical obligations that govern analyst conduct and issuer relationships.

Examination standards are deliberately demanding. Candidates are assessed on their ability to reason through complex analytical scenarios, defend their conclusions with evidence, and apply the Society's adopted ethical frameworks without prompting. Successful candidates are awarded the CEA designation, which serves as the prerequisite for full Membership and, in time, eligibility for Fellowship.

Examinations

Examination Schedule & Global Test Centres

The Society administers CEA examinations on a fixed global schedule, conducted twice annually, so that candidates in every region have consistent, predictable access without compromising the integrity of the assessment process.

Examinations are delivered through a network of accredited test centres in major financial centres. Centres are selected and audited for their capacity to deliver secure, standardised testing conditions, including identity verification, invigilation protocols and technical infrastructure. Results are released on a defined date following each examination window, communicated simultaneously to all candidates globally.

Advanced Standing

Exemptions & Advanced Standing Policy

Exemptions are granted selectively where a candidate demonstrates that prior qualifications or professional experience cover the analytical ground of a given curriculum level with genuine rigour. Recognised pathways include holders of equivalent professional finance credentials, postgraduate degrees in finance from affiliated institutions, and professionals with sustained senior analytical responsibility.

Exemption is never granted on the basis of tenure or seniority alone â€” the assessment focuses on the substance of prior learning, not its duration. Candidates who receive exemption from foundational or intermediate content are still required to demonstrate command of the advanced level in full.

Study Resources

Preparation Providers & Official Study Material

The Society maintains a formal distinction between official study material and third-party preparation content. Official study material is produced or directly commissioned by the Society, developed in coordination with the Global Advisory Committee responsible for curriculum oversight, and mapped precisely to the current syllabus.

The Official Preparation Provider program reviews instructional quality, content accuracy and alignment with the Society's curriculum and ethical standards. Official study materials include a syllabus guide, topic-level learning outcomes, practice question sets calibrated to examination difficulty, and periodic curriculum update bulletins.

Ongoing Competence

Continuing Professional Development (CPD)

The Society regards the CEA designation as a standard that must be maintained, not a credential earned once and carried indefinitely. Members are required to complete defined CPD hours within each reporting cycle, drawn from eligible activities including technical briefings, masterclasses, executive education programs, academic coursework, and structured research activity.

A portion of required CPD hours specifically addresses ethics and professional conduct. Members are subject to periodic audit, and inability to substantiate claimed CPD activity is treated as a compliance matter. CPD obligations apply uniformly across membership grades â€” Fellows are held to the same obligation as Members.

Verification

Digital Badges & Credential Verification

The Society issues digital credentials to members holding the CEA designation, Membership status or Fellowship, providing a verifiable and tamper-resistant means of confirming a member's standing. Each digital badge is cryptographically linked to the Society's central credentialing register and displays the holder's current designation, grade, date of conferral, and standing status.

Verification can be performed independently by any third party through the Society's public verification portal, without requiring the credential holder's involvement. Badge status is updated in real time to reflect compliance, disciplinary proceedings or designation changes.

Scholarly Publication

The International Journal of Equity Analysis

The International Journal of Equity Analysis is the Society's principal scholarly publication â€” peer-reviewed in the fullest sense. Submissions are assessed by practitioners and academics with direct expertise in the subject matter, not by generalist editorial staff seeking publishable volume.

The Journal publishes original research on valuation methodology, market microstructure, disclosure quality, behavioural influences on analyst judgement, and the evolving regulatory frameworks governing research conduct. Submissions must demonstrate methodological soundness and practical relevance â€” the Journal is not an outlet for work that is theoretically elegant but disconnected from applied analysis.

The review process is conducted under double-blind protocol, administered by the Editorial Board in coordination with the relevant Global Advisory Committee. The Journal is published on a quarterly cycle and archived permanently within the Society's research infrastructure.

Market Intelligence

Global Market Outlook Reports

The Society's Global Market Outlook Reports provide members with a structured, cross-regional assessment of equity market conditions, prepared quarterly. These reports address macroeconomic conditions relevant to equity valuation, sector-level developments, regulatory shifts and structural risks that may not yet be reflected in prevailing analytical consensus.

The Society's approach favours measured assessment over prediction. Contributors present reasoned judgement supported by evidence, with appropriate acknowledgment of uncertainty. Regional sections are prepared with input from chapter leadership in the relevant markets, ensuring local regulatory nuance and market structure are represented accurately.

Applied Research

White Papers & Occasional Research Papers

White Papers address emerging methodological questions, structural changes in specific markets or sectors, or the practical implications of regulatory developments as they unfold. Occasional Papers cover retrospective analysis of significant market events, cross-sector comparative studies, and exploratory work on analytical techniques.

Both categories undergo review by the relevant Global Advisory Committee prior to publication. Contributions are accepted from Members, Fellows, academic affiliates and institutional members whose internal research teams have produced work of sufficient quality and relevance.

Professional Standards

Analyst Standards & Best Practice Guidelines

The Society's professional standards incorporate the Research Objectivity Standards (ROS) and the Best Practice Guidelines Governing Analyst/Corporate Issuer Relations in full, supplemented by Society-specific guidance on cross-border research distribution, sector-specific conflicts, and alternative data in research production.

Guidance covers valuation methodology disclosure, handling of forward-looking statements, model assumptions caveats, and documentation standards for institutional research. The Society treats these guidelines as enforceable, with adherence assessed as part of the disciplinary framework.

Access is provided to all Members and Fellows as a standing benefit, with selective access extended to academic affiliates conducting research in partnership with the Society.

Contribute

Call for Papers & Editorial Board

The Society issues periodic Calls for Papers to direct research attention toward areas requiring further scholarly or applied examination. Submissions are accepted from Members, Fellows, academic affiliates and qualified non-members â€” authorship by Society members receives no preferential treatment in the review process.

The Editorial Board oversees the entire publication process. Its members are senior Fellows, academic affiliates and external specialists appointed for defined terms. Board appointments are reviewed periodically to ensure appropriate geographic and sectoral representation.

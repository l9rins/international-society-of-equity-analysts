Formal Statements

Press Room & Media Statements

The Society's Press Room provides accurate, attributable information about the institution's positions, activities and formal statements. Media statements are issued sparingly and only where the Society has a substantive position to communicate: a formal response to regulatory developments, a statement on matters affecting the integrity of equity research, or clarification where public commentary has misrepresented its policies.

Every statement reflects a position formally adopted by the Board of Governors or the relevant Global Advisory Committee. The Press Room archive maintains a chronological record of formal statements, written with the same precision the Society expects of its members' own research â€” precise, evidenced, and free of promotional framing.

The Society does not comment on speculative matters, unresolved internal proceedings, or questions concerning individual members' conduct while under disciplinary review.

Policy Engagement

Regulatory Consultations & Policy Submissions

The Society participates in regulatory consultations as a matter of institutional obligation. Where supervisory bodies seek input on matters bearing on equity research, market conduct or analyst independence, the Society considers a formal submission appropriate â€” provided the matter falls within genuine institutional expertise.

Submissions are prepared under the oversight of the relevant Global Advisory Committee, drawing on practical experience of senior Fellows and external academic affiliates. The Society's positions are not always convenient for its own membership â€” where evidence supports a position that increases compliance burden, the Society takes that position regardless of its reception.

As the Society's global chapter structure develops, submissions will span a range of jurisdictions, reflecting the position that consistent standards require engagement wherever equity markets are regulated.

Analysis & Commentary

The Society Insights Blog

A space for analysis that is timely enough to matter but does not yet warrant the formality of a journal article or white paper. Written by members, Fellows, committee representatives and academic affiliates, held to a standard considerably higher than the term "blog" typically implies.

Content ranges across sector-specific valuation questions, regulatory developments, observations from regional markets, and considered commentary on debates current within the profession. Contributors are expected to take positions rather than summarise consensus.

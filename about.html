Heritage

Our History & Heritage

From inception to institutional maturity — the milestones that shaped the Society.

2018

Formation & Founding Charter

A group of senior practitioners from major financial centres convened to address the growing inconsistency in analytical standards across jurisdictions. The founding charter was drafted and the Society's institutional framework established.

2019

Governance Structure & Ethical Framework

The Board of Governors was constituted and the Society formally adopted the CFA Institute Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct as its ethical foundation, supplemented by the Research Objectivity Standards and Best Practice Guidelines.

2020

CEA Program Design & Curriculum Development

The Credentialing Committee completed the design of the Certified Equity Analyst program, establishing the examination framework, curriculum standards and continuing professional development requirements.

2021

Global Chapter Network & Membership Launch

Regional chapters were established across North America, Europe and Asia-Pacific. The Society opened membership applications across all grades and admitted its inaugural cohort of Associate and Member-level practitioners.

2023

Inaugural CEA Examinations & Fellowship Program

The first CEA examination cycle was administered across 25 test centres globally. The Fellowship grade was introduced and the inaugural cohort of FISEAs was recognised for distinguished contribution to the profession.

2025

Research Publication & Institutional Partnerships

The International Journal of Equity Analysis published its inaugural issue. Strategic partnerships were formalised with leading institutional investors, regulatory bodies and academic affiliates across 15 countries.

2026

Global Recognition & Continued Expansion

The Society now represents over 50,000 members across 190 countries with 25 active chapters. The CEA credential is recognised by leading asset managers, investment banks and regulatory authorities worldwide.

Leadership

Board of Governors & Leadership Council

The Board of Governors serves as the Society's highest governing authority and is responsible for safeguarding the institution's independence, credibility and longâ€‘term direction. It is structured to reflect the standards expected of senior practitioners â€” those who demonstrate sustained professional judgement, ethical resilience and a commitment to the advancement of equity analysis.

Advisory Structure

Global Advisory Committees

The Global Advisory Committees form an important part of the Society's institutional architecture, providing a structured channel through which specialist judgement can be applied to policy, credentialing, research and professional standards. Each committee operates with a defined mandate.

Some focus on curriculum and examination standards, ensuring that the Certified Equity Analyst designation reflects contemporary analytical practice. Others concentrate on regulatory developments, providing structured input on emerging disclosure requirements. Additional committees oversee research priorities, professional ethics, continuing development requirements and the recognition of advanced standing for experienced practitioners.

Committee members are selected for their professional record, independence of judgement and willingness to contribute to the longâ€‘term integrity of the profession. Their work directly influences how standards are set, how examinations evolve, how disciplinary matters are assessed and how the Society positions itself in global policy discussions.

Global Presence

Global Chapters & Regional Offices

The Society's global orientation reflects a deliberate commitment to maintaining professional standards across jurisdictions while recognising the distinct regulatory, cultural and market characteristics that shape different financial centres.

Chapters are led by practitioners who understand both local market dynamics and the expectations of an international professional body. Their role is custodial rather than administrative, ensuring that credentialing, continuing development, disciplinary processes and member engagement are executed with consistency and integrity.

The Society's approach to global expansion is intentionally selective. Formal representation is introduced only where professional need, regulatory alignment and the presence of practitioners capable of upholding the Society's standards justify it.
